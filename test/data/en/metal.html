Silver Going On 3rd Consecutive Annual Shortfall, Should Be Price Supportive:Thomson Reuters GFMS

Nov 19, 2015 09:23 GMT   Source:scrap monster

Tags: precious metals,  silver prices

By Paul Ploumis (ScrapMonster Author)
November 19, 2015 04:41:25 AM
The silver market is expected to be in an annual physical deficit of 42.7 Moz in 2015, said Thomson Reuters GFMS in its Silver Interim Report released Tuesday evening.Silver Going On 3rd Consecutive Annual Shortfall, Should Be Price Supportive:Thomson Reuters GFMS
(Kitco News) - The silver market is heading towards a third consecutive year of a physical supply crunch, and although such deficits do not affect prices in the short run, it may push the metal’s price higher longer term, says one leading precious metals research firm.

“The silver market is expected to be in an annual physical deficit of 42.7 Moz in 2015, marking the third consecutive year the market has realized an annual physical shortfall,” said Thomson Reuters GFMS in its Silver Interim Report released Tuesday evening.

“While such deficits do not necessarily influence prices in the near term, multiple years of annual deficits can begin to apply upward pressure to prices in subsequent periods,” analysts added.

Thomson Reuters GFMS noted that silver priced averaged $15.51 an ounce, which represents an 18.3% decline from the same period last year.

“The GFMS team at Thomson Reuters forecasts silver prices to average $15.51/oz for the full calendar year,” the report said.

December Comex silver futures have been declining for 15 consecutive trading sessions and were last quoted down just under 1% at $14.03 an ounce. 

According to the report, total silver supply is forecast to fall to 1,014.4 million ounces in 2015, down 3% from the previous year. “This decline is expected to be driven by flat mine production, a 5% drop in scrap return, and net de-hedging of 12.6 Moz,” the report said.

Erica Rannestad, senior precious metals demand analyst for the firm, told Kitco News that the biggest theme in the silver market over the medium term will be mine production.

“[Mine production] is swinging from very positive growth over the past 5-10 years to what we’re expecting to be a projected decline. So, that’s going to start to apply more upward pressure to prices,” she said. “The reason is that these shortfalls are going to start eat away at the above stock levels,” she explained.

The report showed that mine production is expected to total 867.2 million ounces in 2015, up 0.3% from a year ago, but would also be its weakest performance since 2002.

Despite a surge in physical demand, particularly in bullion coin sales, Rannestad said she thinks it is going to “more of a supply story” for silver prices over the medium term.

“Silver coin demand is forecast to increase 21% in 2015 to total a record high of 129.9 Moz,” the report said. “Coin demand should account for 12% of physical demand this year, up from 10% in 2014 and just 4% ten years ago.”

However, Rannestad noted that she anticipates a “not too material” drop in coin demand from the record levels reached in 2015.

Overall though, the firm expects total physical demand to fall.

“Total physical demand is forecast to contract by 2.5% in 2015, to 1,057.1 Moz, primarily driven by a 12.9 Moz drop in electronics demand,” the report said.

Demand from electronics sector has been in decline since 2011, the report continued, mainly driven by the trend towards “consumer electronics miniaturization” as well as a weaker economy in China.

As for the effects of tightening U.S. monetary policy, Rannestad said a lot of market participants feel that rate hikes have been priced into the market already.

“You may see an initial reaction but it will be very transient, and [silver’s] trajectory will reassert itself in the pattern that it went before,” she said. “[Silver] will take its cues from gold .”
