Rod Stewart faked his driving test

Dipesh Gadher Published: 20 January 2013
  • Print

Rod Stewart, who confessed his roadie took his original driving test for him (Jim Dyson)

DO YA think I’m roadworthy? Rod Stewart has confessed that a roadie took his driving test for him, allowing the singer to acquire a licence under false pretences.

Stewart, whose hits include Do Ya Think I’m Sexy? and Maggie May, committed the fraud in 1967 and carried on driving until he moved to America eight years later and took a driving test there. He now has a valid US licence which can be used in Britain.

The revelation was in Stewart’s autobiography, published last autumn, but went unnoticed even by the newspaper that serialised the book. Highlights about his passion for cars appear in the Driving section of today’s Sunday Times.

Stewart, 68, recalls how a road manager, Pete Saunders, volunteered to sit the driving test after becoming fed up with having to ferry the singer around London.
