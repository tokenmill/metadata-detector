Extensive CCTV plots Bayley's movements

An extended timeline of CCTV footage has been released which follows Jill Meagher as well as Adrian Bayley, the man accused of her rape and murder.

The chilling CCTV footage of Adrian Ernest Bayley's movements in the hours before and after he raped and allegedly murdered Jill Meagher are a small glimpse of a painstaking and careful police investigation.

The summary of evidence, also released to the media on Tuesday, reveals that Bayley and his girlfriend begin to argue about his possessiveness and jealousy. The friend calls it a night and leaves in a taxi. 

Released to the media by a magistrate on Tuesday, the footage - from multiple cameras at multiple locations across Melbourne - could never explain why Bayley did what he is alleged to have done. But it is a ghastly and compelling illustration to go with the dry words of the police summary of evidence.

The accused: Adrian Ernest Bayley Photo: Supplied

It begins at 5.41pm on Friday, September 21, with Bayley standing at a National Australia Bank ATM. He is wearing 'snap off' tracksuit pants, a blue hoody and a beanie. His face is impassive, as he stares off to his left, down the footpath.

The next footage of Bayley is at the Quiet Man hotel, on Flemington Road in Flemington. At 7.28pm, with his then-girlfriend, Bayley walks through the doors of the Irish-themed pub, where the pipe-laying firm he works for is holding a function.

Looking like any 40-something man gearing up for Friday night drinks with friends or workmates, he is wearing a light-grey shirt, jeans and white shoes.

The victim: Jill Meagher.

Jump forward two-and-a-half hours, and the same camera captures Bayley and his girlfriend going to leave the pub, when Bayley is distracted by a colleague. Laughing, he holds the colleague by the back of the neck and leans in close to him, talking into his ear.

Bayley, his girlfriend and the other man then walk out of the pub onto Flemington Road, where they eventually hail a cab and drive off towards the city.

Soon afterwards, they arrive at the Lounge on Swanston St, a late-night institution and onetime haunt of students from the University of Melbourne and RMIT.

Arrested: Adrian Ernest Bayley hides his face from the media as he is taken into custody. Photo: Luis Enrique Ascui

The summary of evidence, also released to the media on Tuesday, reveals that Bayley and his girlfriend begin to argue about his possessiveness and jealousy. The friend calls it a night and leaves in a taxi.

Bayley's girlfriend tells him she his going to toilet, then slips out of the bar and also hails a taxi, which takes her back to the Coburg house she shares with Bayley and others.

At 12.24am, a camera over the bar at the Lounge captures images of Bayley pacing up and down and holding his phone to his ear, then the phone's screen glowing as Bayley peers intently at it.

Jill Meagher crime scene photos

Police forensic photos tendered to court in the case of Jill Meagher's rape and killing. Photo: Supplied

The summary says that at this point, Bayley is repeatedly phoning and texting his girlfriend to find out where she is.

Cameras in the stairwell of the second-floor venue then record Bayley walking down the stairs and out onto Swanston Street, where he has a brief conversation with the bouncer, then walks towards Little Bourke Street.

According to the summary, Bayley then catches a taxi home, pulls on the blue hooded top so familiar from the footage police initially released, and travels to Brunswick "by unknown means".

Seventy minutes after Bayley leaves the Lounge, a camera in a real estate agency on Sydney Road records Ms Meagher walking north. Thirty seconds later, Bayley strides after her. It is the first footage linking the two.

Soon afterwards comes the by-now infamous vision of Bayley talking to Ms Meagher outside the Duchess Boutique, near the corner of Sydney Road and Hope Street. It is shortly after this that Bayley raped and allegedly murdered Ms Meagher in a laneway off Hope Street.

Bayley then goes back to his Coburg home and puts a shovel in his white Holden Astra, according to the police summary.

Grainy CCTV vision shows the Astra entering the laneway at 4.22am and then leaving at 4.25am.

The summary alleges that with Ms Meagher's body in the boot of the car, Bayley drives south towards Royal Parade and then west towards Citylink, where a gantry camera near Moreland Road captures the Astra heading north at 4.40am.

At approximately 5am, according to the summary, Bayley stopped his car on Black Hill Road, Gisborne South, took Ms Meagher's body from the boot and buried it in a 35-centimetre-deep hole.

He got back in the car and started driving back towards the Calder Highway, but ran out of petrol. An unsuspecting motorist stopped to help, agreeing to drive Bayley to a Caltex service station in Sunbury.

Footage from the CCTV camera there shows Bayley, dressed in tracksuit pants, a hooded top and a beanie, climbing out of the blue ute at 6.16am and entering the service station shop. He selects a round petrol tin, goes out to the pump and fills the tin up, before coming back into the shop and paying for the fuel.

He goes back outside, looks around briefly, then gets back into the ute, which then pulls out. The driver dropped Bayley back at the broken-down Astra, and continued on his way.

It is unclear from the summary how Bayley passed the rest of that day after he returned to his Coburg home, but a camera captured him at a service station in Fawkner just before 10pm on Saturday, wearing shorts and thongs. Police allege he cleaned the car inside and out on Sunday.

The last of the footage released on Tuesday shows Bayley walking into a Fawkner tyre shop, where police allege he had all four tyres changed, paying $200 in cash.
