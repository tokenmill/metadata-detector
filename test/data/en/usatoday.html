LeBron James, Heat too much for much-improved Lakers

David Leon Moore, USA TODAY Sports2:23a.m. EST January 18, 2013

Heat forward LeBron James drives on Lakers center Dwight Howard during Thursday's 99-90 win.(Photo: Jayne Kamin-Oncea, USA TODAY Sports)

Story Highlights

  • Miami Heat beat Los Angeles Lakers 99-90 Thursday night
  • Lakers, calling game "measuring stick," showed improvement behind solid defense, keeping game close
  • LeBron James scored 39 points on 17-for-25 shooting, added eight assists, seven rebounds for Heat

LOS ANGELES — In Game 3 of the Los Angeles Lakers' self-proclaimed "new season," did they prove they could play defense against an elite team?

Yes.

Did they prove they could stick with an elite team most of the way?

Yes.

Did they prove they could beat an elite team?

No. Not an elite team with LeBron James on it, anyway.

James (39 points on 17-for-25 shooting), Dwyane Wade (27 points) and Ray Allen hit clutch shots down the stretch to propel the defending champion Miami Heat to a 99-90 victory Thursday night.

BOX SCORE: Heat 99, Lakers 90

"Just a phenomenal player," Kobe Bryant said of James. "One of the best we've ever seen."

The Heat (26-12), the defending NBA champs, were playing the last game of a six-game road trip on which they have struggled. The win gave them a 3-3 mark on the trip.

"It was very important for us," James said. "We want to continue to get better and end this trip on the right foot. This was a good, quality win."

The Lakers won their previous two games, drubbing the Cleveland Cavaliers and Milwaukee Bucks. Bryant called Thursday's matchup with the Heat "a measuring stick" for what the Lakers, who recently lost six games in a row to fall to 15-21, believe is a much-improved product.

The verdict: Yes, they're improved, and they played the Heat to a standstill — 83-83 — through 3½ quarters, with the Staples Center faithful rocking with renewed hope.

But the Lakers fell to 17-22, confident they're an improving team but still out of the current playoff picture.

"We've just got to keep motoring, keep pushing," Bryant said.

Bryant, the league's leading scorer who told USA TODAY Sports Wednesday that winning a third scoring title this year means nothing to him, played a facilitator role early in the game, passing up open perimeter shots to pass the ball inside to Dwight Howard and others.

When he started shooting, he was cold, and he finished with 22 points, missing 17 of 25 shots, though he did tie it at 90 with a three-pointer with 2:12 left.

ALL-STAR SNUBS: Who should the fans have voted in?

"I allowed myself to be too much of a decoy," Bryant said. "I should have been more aggressive from the start."

The opening minutes couldn't have gone much worse for the Lakers, as they committed turnover after turnover and the Heat scored their first eight points on dunks, two by James and two by Wade.

But the Lakers weathered that storm and settled into something of a defensive battle — Lakers? Mike D'Antoni? Defensive battle? — for the rest of the half.

The Lakers continued to struggle with taking care of the ball -- they committed 16 first-half turnovers, including four each by Steve Nash and Bryant -- but outshot and outrebounded the Heat and took a 45-44 lead into the halftime break.

KOBE BEATS LEBRON: In All-Star voting, at least

Pretty amazing, actually. If the Lakers were told before the game that at halftime Bryant would be outscored by James 18-6 and they would turn the ball over 16 times and would score just 45 points, they might figure they'd be trailing by 10 or 15.

Then again, if they had committed 10 fewer turnovers, they might have led by 10 at halftime.

"We should have had some separation," D'Antoni said. "The turnovers just killed us. Ninety-nine (points) against the best offensive team in the league, that's good enough (defense) to win."

Before the game, D'Antoni left himself a lot of wiggle room about just how big this game was for the Lakers.

"It's not the end of the world if it doesn't work out," he said. "It's always a test against the champions . . . We'll spin it some way to our benefit."

Here was the post-game spin: "I thought we played well enough to win. We just have to keep getting better defensively and smooth out our offense."
