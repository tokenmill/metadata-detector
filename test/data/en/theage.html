AFL clubs respond to our survey on drug use and illegal practices

Date
February 9, 2013
  • (0)
  • Tweet
  • submit to reddit
  • Email article
  • Print
  • Reprints & permissions

Fairfax Media asked each AFL club the following questions:

  1. Have any of your club officials or players been interviewed or contacted by the Australian Crime Commission or police acting on ACC intelligence?

  2. Do your players take supplements or vitamins orally or by injection?

  3. Do you require players to sign waivers, consent forms or non-disclosure agreements regarding any such treatments?

  4. Advertisement

    Do your players undergo injections or intravenous treatments administered by staff other than qualified medical personnel?

  5. Are you confident organised criminals have not infiltrated your club?   

  6. Are you confident your players and staff have not acted illegally?

 

ADELAIDE FOOTBALL CLUB CHAIRMAN AND ACTING CHIEF EXECUTIVE ROB CHAPMAN 

The Adelaide Football club acknowledges the gravity of the Australian Crime Commission’s report findings. The Club fully supports the investigations and the AFL’s response, which we think is comprehensive and clearly signals a new sense of urgency which we subscribe to.

We will assist the AFL in any way to ensure the integrity of the game and our club is upheld.

There is a considerable saturation of media commentary on this issue and we can confirm that we have had no information sought or conveyed to us relating to the AFL or indeed the club. 

All our club medical and physical conditioning staff have been consulted following recent events and the club is in the process of conducting our own analysis, but we believe their conduct is consistent and compliant with AFL guidelines. We will continue to review all club systems and procedures.

No one can ever be 100 per cent certain that their staff and players are not involved, but given the significant education, welfare and support that is provided by us to our staff and players we have every confidence that we are compliant.

 

CARLTON FOOTBALL CLUB SPOKESMAN 

  1. No.

  2. Orally.

  3. No.

  4. No.

  5. Yes.

  6. Yes.

The club will work with the AFL and co-operate fully with the audit process. 

 

COLLINGWOOD FOOTBALL CLUB SPOKESMAN 

The Collingwood Football Club will not be responding to your questions.

 

FREMANTLE FOOTBALL CLUB 

  1. As far as we are aware, no.

  2. Our club has a food-first focus and supplements (Australian-made only) are a part of that process but they are only a minor part of it. Supplements are ingested orally. Vitamin B injections are administered only under strict medical advice and on a very limited basis.

  3. No.

  4. No.

  5. Yes.

  6. Yes.

 

GEELONG CATS CLUB CHIEF EXECUTIVE BRIAN COOK

The club is fully committed to assisting the AFL and ASADA in this investigation. We have nothing to hide and there has been no wrong doing on our part. The club’s doctors, and not any sport science staff, directly determine all treatments and the use of all supplements by players. No player has ever been requested to sign a waiver or indemnity concerning supplements used.

 

HAWTHORN FOOTBALL CLUB SPOKESWOMAN

Hawthorn Football Club is confident of the integrity of our highly regarded conditioning and medical staff, and of our focus on governance. We will fully cooperate with the AFL Commission and Executive when required, but do not believe any further comment is appropriate until that process has occurred.

MELBOURNE FOOTBALL CLUB SPOKESWOMAN 

The use of supplements are common practice in high-performance sport and they are used as part of Melbourne’s training regime. They are strictly monitored and used in accordance with ASADA protocols. 

 

NORTH MELBOURNE FOOTBALL CLUB SPOKESMAN 

We welcome the upcoming AFL audit and have no issues or concerns with the fitness, conditioning and medical programs in place at North Melbourne.

 

RICHMOND SPOKESMAN 

The AFL will be conducting an audit with every club, and we look forward to working with them to ensure our practices are compliant.

 

ST KILDA HEAD OF FOOTBALL CHRIS PELCHEN

All training supplements used by the St Kilda Football Club are AFL-approved products and supplied by reputable companies.

No training supplement or medication is provided to the players by the St Kilda Football Club without prior approval of our Head Physician.

No St Kilda player has been asked to sign a waiver in relation to the use of training supplements or medication.

 

SYDNEY SWANS SPOKESMAN

  1. We are not aware of any club official or player being interviewed or contacted by the ACC or police. We are fully supportive of the AFL stance on protecting the integrity of our game and would fully cooperate with any investigations if required.  

  2. Like most elite athletes, our players do take approved vitamins and supplements in consultation with our dietician and club doctor. 

  3. No.

  4. No. All treatment is overseen by our club doctor.

  5. We have no reason to believe organised crime has infiltrated the club.

  6. We are confident in our policies and procedures and have no reason to believe our staff or players have acted illegally.

 

WESTERN BULLDOGS CHIEF EXECUTIVE SIMON GARLICK 

The Western Bulldogs  are absolutely committed to protecting the integrity of our great game. We have in place a range of rigorous processes to meet our responsibilities under the law and under the anti-doping code.

Every player is educated annually on their requirements under the anti-doping code.

We do not require players to sign waivers, consent forms or non-disclosure agreements regarding any treatment.

 

No response was received from:

Brisbane Lions 

Gold Coast Suns 

Essendon

West Coast Eagles
